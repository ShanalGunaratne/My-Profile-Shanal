My Project Idea

Project: Overview

My project will be an application called the "Fuel Limit" that will be able to ration and allocate appropriate amounts of petroleum to the population of a country. Simply, it will record a citizen's consumption of petrol and make sure everyone keeps to their limit. Firstly, every citizen will be allowed to register and access an account that will provide them with information about their petrol usage. Then they will be able to use their mobile app or barcode to purchase their allocated ration. Anyone that cannot access the mobile application will be allowed to access the system with their national identity card at the gas station. This is made for the government to regulate and support the economy.

Project: Motivation

Currently, my country Sri Lanka is going through an economic crisis and is running out of essential things such as electricity, petroleum, medicine, and many more. One of the most problematic things during this time is fuel queues. These queues are growing longer every day, and some have created new queues next to the old ones, shutting down roads and increasing traffic. People are now getting angry and frustrated, leading to fights with gas station employees and law enforcement. However, the most worrying thing is that people have started to steal from others by harming vehicles, leading to many accidents and extremely dangerous situations. My application "Fuel Limit " will be able to ration every citizen's usage accordingly and lawfully stop any dangerous activity.

Project: Description

Fuel Limit will be accessible as a website or as a mobile application. First, the user will need to register to the application using their vehicle registration number, chassis number, number plate and other essential information required by the Motor Traffic Department. Once the user's identity has been verified, he will be allowed a quota on a weekly basis. If the user has any problems with the quota, they will need to lodge an application explaining why they should be given a higher limit. For example, if the users are ambulance drivers, they will be allowed a higher limit. According to users’ requirements and if their application need is seen as essential, their weekly quotas will be increased.

Once a user's limit is decided, they will be able to purchase petrol at gas stations. The purchasing process can be carried out in two ways. Users will be able to load a QR (quick response) code that will be scanned by the gas station worker. Once the user's details are verified, they will be able to access their given ration of petrol. In addition to QR codes, users are given the option of using Bluetooth beacons and NFC tags. To use the NFC tag the user needs to bring their device and tap the scanner and the Bluetooth beacon will allow the scanner to access the user's data as long as they are near to them. These options will allow users to stay safe from COVID-19. Once the purchase is made the system will update the purchase in the system. The second purchasing process is created when a customer does not have a smartphone. May it be they do not own one or forgot to bring it, they will have to go through a long process to verify their identity and current usage. The gas station worker will ask for their national identity number and the pin number given during registration.

The gas station employee will also be able to access the system to regulate the quantity of fuel in storage. They will be given a scanner that will scan QR codes, detect NFC tags and receive signals from a Bluetooth beacon. After the scan, they will be able to access the remaining usage of the customer. Then they will refuel the vehicle accordingly and update the system. Once the system updates the customer usage, after it is reduced, it will not change until the next week. 

It will also include normal features like push notifications and important news regarding fuel from the government, security to access the application, speed, and simplicity. It also has a function to derive feedback and depending on the needs, the updates will be created. This application not only provides a service to the customers but also the country's management. The government will be able to access the overall usage of petrol by its citizens. This data will be useful in multiple ways. For example, with real-total usage, the next shipment can be calculated and ordered faster.

Project: Tools and Technologies

For this application, a few languages will be used. SQL, as it involves government data from all its citizens. Next, we will use the open-source software called Flutter to allow android and IOS users to access the application. In order to facilitate the purchasing options, such as the NFC tags, QR codes and Bluetooth beacons, a proper scanner must be used. This scanner needs to be small and somewhat portable.

Project: Skills Required  

First, we need to write and create the application using Flutter and SQL. To do this we need a team of software developers that can work with the government to create a unique product for the country. One of the main issues will be to get the agreement of the government. Once acceptance is granted, we can create the application by working together with the government. After the software is created, we will need a QR scanner that will need to be remodeled with NFC tags and Bluetooth beacon acceptance hardware. If we are able to get the agreement of the government, the ability and resources to create this application will also be solved.

Project: Outcome

If this application is built and implemented, we will not see any large queues or congested roads. We will be able to put a stop to the destruction of property and illegal extra consumption and selling of petrol. Every citizen will be allowed the bare minimum of what they need to carry out their work and daily activities. Users will be able to go back to their previous schedules, and economic activity and productivity will start to move again, which in turn will help the economy. The government will be able to order the next shipment more accurately and will be able to focus its resources on other economic issues.
